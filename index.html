Our Philosophy

Product is the priority. Research makes it stronger.

At Obsidian Labs, we begin with what can become useful, durable, and deployable. Research is essential to that process, but product remains the priority because real systems are where intelligence is tested, refined, and proven.

01. Curiosity, Then Utility

We investigate what matters.

We still begin with questions about how intelligence can reason, adapt, and collaborate, but we study them with an eye toward what can eventually become useful in real systems.

02. Products Are The Proof

Ideas must survive deployment.

We do not treat products as side effects of research. A capability only becomes meaningful when it can be engineered, deployed, and trusted in practice.

03. Research Must Withstand Scrutiny

Only strong ideas should move forward.

We value rigorous, reproducible work and deliberate review. If an idea cannot survive scrutiny, it does not belong inside systems we expect others to depend on.

04. Deployment Feeds The Process

Shipping teaches us what research cannot.

Every system we deploy creates new evidence. Product is not the end of the process for us, it feeds back into research and sharpens what we build next.

Research -> Product Process

How the lab turns discovery into usable systems.

Our process is designed to keep research ambitious without letting it drift into isolated experiments. Every capability is forced through a process of validation, system design, and real deployment pressure.

01. Discovery

Research the capability.

We begin with model behavior, new architectures, and experimental patterns that could become reusable intelligence primitives.

02. Validation

Stress-test the claim.

Capabilities must survive benchmarking, failure review, and grounded evaluation before we treat them as something the platform can trust.

03. Systemization

Turn it into infrastructure.

We translate validated behavior into shared layers like memory, retrieval, tool use, orchestration, and multimodal interfaces.

04. Deployment

Push it into products.

The capability is applied inside real workflows where latency, trust, usability, and operational complexity expose what still needs work.

05. Feedback Process

Feed reality back into research.

What breaks, scales, or surprises us in production informs the next round of research so the lab compounds instead of restarting.

Research

The research behind the systems we ship.

Our lab keeps a live research agenda across core intelligence systems. Some tracks are still under active investigation, some have already shaped deployed products, and some define the long-range domains we continue to invest in.

01. Research Domain

Reasoning and planning systems

We study how intelligence can break down problems, maintain structured thought across multiple steps, and make decisions that remain stable under ambiguity.

Focus: decomposition, control, search, long-form decision flow
Why it matters: this domain shapes the core systems behind deliberate, high-context AI behavior.
02. Research Domain

Memory and retrieval infrastructure

This work explores how systems remember, retrieve, compress, and rank information so intelligence can stay continuous across sessions, tools, and evolving tasks.

Focus: context persistence, recall quality, retrieval assembly
Why it matters: better memory makes agent systems feel cumulative rather than stateless.
03. Research Domain

Multimodal interface cognition

We investigate how models interpret screens, documents, images, and structured environments together so intelligence can operate in the formats real work actually uses.

Focus: document reasoning, interface state, vision-language alignment
Why it matters: useful systems must understand more than text prompts alone.
04. Research Domain

Verification and grounded generation

This domain focuses on how outputs are checked, traced, and grounded so systems remain trustworthy when answers carry operational or reputational weight.

Focus: evaluation layers, citation logic, failure review
Why it matters: capability without verification is not enough for dependable deployment.
05. Research Domain

Tool use and agent orchestration

We research how intelligent systems plan actions, coordinate tools, and manage execution chains without losing reliability, control, or human oversight.

Focus: action planning, tool routing, multi-step execution
Why it matters: orchestration is what turns raw model ability into useful workflow automation.
06. Research Domain

Efficient deployment and optimization

This area studies how to lower latency, reduce cost, and improve runtime efficiency without giving up the quality and reliability expected from frontier systems.

Focus: runtime design, compression, inference efficiency
Why it matters: research only compounds when it can be deployed at useful speed and scale.
